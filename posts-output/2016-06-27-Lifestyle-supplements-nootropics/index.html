Oxidative stress: its causes, its consequences and its solution

I used to get the flu twice a year in average, and many more common colds. Since I started taking chinese green tea and, later, other supplements, my cold and flu have gone away for good. This is nothing but a noticeable consequece mainly of oxidative stress.

Causes

The human body, and that of any other living creature, is essentially a water bag where a lot of organic chemical reactions happen. This reactions have a residue footprint which needs to be taken care of. This residue my be of the form of free electrons, which will be looking for a positively-charged molecule to bind to. Another harmful residue type is the so-called free radicals, which are incomplete molecules, or their combining heads, which are able to bind to other molecules in a chemical reaction in place of the intended molecule, since they share the combining part. The resulting molecule is not, however, the desired one.

If a molecule which itself needs to exist as it is in order to give raise to another chemical reaction is oxidized or a free radical happens to be in a location where a chemical reaction where the compatible molecule is intended to be used, this second reaction may not occur or, worse, it may fail to produce the intended result.

Another way of getting oxidative stress is through exogenous administration of toxins or other oxidants. These may come in the diet and become oxidizers once their are at a specific location within the human body.

Consequences

Since oxidizers prevent chemical reactions from obtaining the desired product, the harmful effect is two-fold:

  • Organic stores are depleted, since some material is wasted in a processwith an unwanted result
  • This unwanted product may end up being toxic for the surrounding tissue,itself preventing other processes from producing the intended result.

Oxidizers harm cell membranes, including neurons (which makes them one possible cause for Alzheimer's), they oxidize other molecules such as proteins, DNA, RNA, and so forth. Thus, we might not get enough proteins from DNA transcription, making us sick in a number of ways if we reach a threshold. Normal processes are hampered, including the ones needed for immunology, which weakens our defences against disease. If DNA is harmed beyond repair, a cancer might be born, and if natural cancer fighting mechanisms (apoptosis, for example) may not be enough to stop it, which happens if our immunologic system is weak.

Solution

Exercise is an excellent way of getting rid of toxins. However, a good diet contains many of the antioxidants needed for the everyday activity.

Green tea

The secret of green tea is its antioxidants. Green tea is high in cathechins and polyphenols, two types of antioxidant molecules, which means they reduce oxidative stress resulting from the normal biological activity of the body (and increased by dietary bad practice).

One of the catechins, EGCg (Epigallocatechin gallate), is known to be a powerful free radical scavenger, antioxidant, and NGF (Nerve Growth Factor) inducer/amplifier, which promotes the growth of neurons at any age and keeps our brain healthy.

Vitamin C

Vitamin C is the most common water-soluble antioxidant and one of the most studied. Vitamin C comes in several forms, having the Ascorbate radical in common (which means absence of scurvy). The most common form, ascorbic acid, is acidic, so that the stomach will have a limit in the amount it can tolerate before provoking burps and belly moves. Sodium ascorbate is a salt form which is gentler to the stomach. Studies show that the amount orally taken and the amount that is absorbed are not proportional, but follows some kind of logarithmic relationship $absorbed = log(taken)$. Also, much of it is destroyed in the stomach. For both the previous reasons, Vitamin C is best taken in small doses over the day.

Liposomal C

Liposomal C is Internet hype which is not liposomal at all. Liposomes are fatty capsules that go through the stomach, the intestine walls and the bloods and that fuse with cell walls, liberating their content within cells.

Liposomes are too expensive and complicated to be built with house equipment as the hype claims. What you can achieve with an ultrasound machine and lecithin is emulsified vitamin C, which means that it will bind to a both fat-water-soluble emulsifier (lecithin), making it more ready to be absorbed by the intestines. So, despite being a leap in what absorption has to do, they reach cells no easier than any other form of Vitamin C.

Vitamin E

Vitamin E is a fat-soluble antioxidant. Apart from its antioxidant activity, Vitamin E is an antiviral that kills the HPV (Human Papilomavirus), which affects the skin by growing strange artifacts, including warts. As a fat-soluble molecule, Vitamin E can be stored in the liver so consumption can be made at any time but excess can build up and reach toxic amounts.

Selenium

Selenium is a powerful antioxidant which also helps thyroid function and brain development. Selenium can take free electrons, relieving surrounding tissue.

Lutein and carothenoids

Lutein is the most common carothenoid in the brain and eyes. It has neuroprotective capabilities.

Other carothenoids are especially present in carrots and are excellent antioxidants.
